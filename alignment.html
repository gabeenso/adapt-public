Alignment Meeting
Talking Points

A 15-minute structured conversation to diagnose fit, identify the right pathway, and agree the next step.

Format: Video call or in-person
Duration: 15 mins structured + 15 mins open
Internal use only
Primary objective
Answer three questions by the end of the call: (1) Do they genuinely serve the right kind of SME owners? (2) Are they better suited to Alliance or Charter right now? (3) Is there enough fit to send the agreement?
Before the call
Pre-call checklist
Review their website and LinkedIn — what do they do, who do they serve, how big are they?
Check for SME advisory focus — strategy, leadership, succession, governance, growth?
Note any mutual connections — warm introductions land better if you can reference a shared contact
Check for competing frameworks — are they already deploying EOS, Scaling Up, or similar?
Have the landing page open — theadaptway.com/partners — in case they want a visual walkthrough
Have both agreement templates ready to send — Alliance or Charter, depending on outcome
The conversation
1
Set context
1–2 minutes
Frame the call as a two-way conversation — you're both working out if this is a fit. Don't pitch. Set the tone: peer to peer, not seller to buyer.
"Thanks for making time. I reached out because you work closely with SME owners and you're probably seeing some of the same structural issues we see — owner dependence, patchy alignment, no clean path to handover. We've built a partner program around solving those problems, and I mostly want to work out whether either of our two pathways would be useful for your firm. Happy if you ask me anything along the way."
2
Discovery
6–7 minutes
Let them talk. You're listening for: the types of businesses they serve, the depth of their SME owner relationships, and whether their current model has a recurring revenue gap. Don't ask all of these — pick the ones that matter based on what you already know from prep.
  • Who are your best clients today?
  • What kinds of privately held businesses do you work with most?
  • Where do you already get traction — strategy, leadership, succession, finance, growth, governance?
  • How much of your firm's revenue still depends on the right people being in the room?
  • How important is recurring revenue or a stickier offer over the next 12–24 months?
3
Pressure points
3–4 minutes
Test whether they're seeing the same problems Adapt solves. If they can't describe these issues from their own client work, the partnership won't have enough gravity.
  • What owner issues do you hear most often right now?
  • How often do succession, handover, owner dependence, or leadership alignment come up?
  • Do your clients need a more structured operating system after the initial advisory work?
    Listen for: "We do the strategy, but it doesn't stick" or "They keep pulling the owner back in."
4
Partnership appetite
2–3 minutes
Now test depth of interest. Are they warm-door openers, or do they want to embed this into their offer? Don't lead them — let them tell you where they sit.
  • Would you rather start by opening a few warm doors, or are you actively looking for something deeper to embed?
  • How much internal capacity do you have to take on certification and activation in the next 90 days?
  • Would you be open to co-hosting a conversation or event with us?
5
Close and next step
1–2 minutes
Make a clear recommendation. Name the pathway. State the specific next step. Don't leave it vague.
If Alliance: "Based on what you've described, Alliance feels like the right starting point. You keep doing what you do, open a few doors where there's real fit, and we handle the rest. I'll send the agreement through today — it's short and plain-English. Have a read and let me know if anything doesn't sit right."
If Charter: "It sounds like you're ready for something deeper — Charter would give you the platform, the certification, and the recurring revenue stream. I'll send the agreement and onboarding pack through within 24 hours. Take your time with it, but the 90-day activation starts once you sign — so the sooner you're in, the sooner you're earning."
If unclear: "I think there's a fit here but I want to be honest — I'm not sure which pathway is right yet. How about I send you both agreements to read side by side, and we have a quick 10-minute follow-up once you've had time to think about which model suits your firm better?"
Pathway recommendation
Quick decision matrix
→ Recommend Alliance
  • "We already have the relationships; we just want a better way to help with these problems."
  • "We don't want a big new delivery model yet, but we can definitely open warm doors."
  • "A trusted referral path and occasional co-hosted conversation would make sense."
  • They have strong SME relationships but limited capacity to embed right now.
Next step: Send Alliance agreement same day.
→ Recommend Charter
  • "We want something stickier and more recurring than one-off projects."
  • "We're already doing adjacent advisory work and could embed this properly."
  • "We'd take certification seriously and want to onboard clients, not just refer them."
  • They have capacity, they're outcome-focused, and they want to invest.
Next step: Send Charter agreement + onboarding pack within 24hrs.

Remember: Alliance Partners can graduate to Charter when signals are right — typically two Design referrals that convert, or one strong embedded client relationship.

Red flags — don't proceed
  • They mainly want passive income or software resale
  • They cannot describe the SME owner problems they solve
  • They want exclusivity before proving fit
  • They light up on the economics and go flat on client outcomes
  • They're already running a competing framework (EOS, Scaling Up, etc.) and can't see how they'd coexist
